Because NumPy is the "engine" that powers scientific computing in Python, it is used everywhere that involves heavy numbers or massive datasets.

Here are the primary industries and companies where NumPy is currently in use (as of 2026):

## 1. Top Tech Companies

- **Google & Meta:** Use NumPy for **AI Research** and processing large-scale data for search algorithms and social media feeds.
    
- **Netflix & Spotify:** Power their **Recommendation Engines**. When Netflix suggests a movie, it uses NumPy (via Pandas/Scikit-learn) to calculate the mathematical "distance" between your tastes and a movie's features.
    
- **Amazon:** Uses it for **Inventory Forecasting** and logistics optimization to ensure products are in the right warehouse at the right time.
    

## 2. Space & Science

- **NASA:** Uses NumPy for analyzing **Mission Data**, telescope images, and simulations. When you see a high-res photo of a black hole or a Martian landscape, NumPy arrays were likely used to stitch those pixels together.
    
- **Pharma (e.g., Pfizer):** Used in **Clinical Trial Analytics** and molecular modeling to predict how different drug compounds will interact.
    

## 3. Finance & Banking

- **JPMorgan Chase & Goldman Sachs:** Use it for **Risk Management** and high-frequency trading models. It helps them calculate "Value at Risk" (VaR) by processing millions of market scenarios in seconds.
    
- **Fraud Detection:** Banks use NumPy to process transactional data in real-time to flag unusual patterns (like a purchase in a country you've never visited).
    

## 4. Engineering & Creative Tech

- **Autonomous Vehicles (Tesla/Uber):** Used for **Signal Processing** and sensor data. The car’s cameras and LIDAR feed data into NumPy arrays to "see" the road.
    
- **Image/Video Editing:** Since images are just 3D arrays of numbers (Red, Green, Blue), NumPy is used to apply filters, change brightness, or compress video files.
